      <h2>FEA Smartphone Chassis Design</h2>

      <div class="meta">
        <p><strong>Summary:</strong></p>
        <p>The goal of this assignment was to design a lightweight, premium smartphone chassis with high bending and torsional stiffness while maintaining safe operating temperatures under CPU and battery heat loads.</p>
        <p>SolidWorks was used to generate and refine the geometry, run finite element analysis (FEA) structural and thermal simulations, and render final design images. Microsoft Excel and MATLAB were used to conduct the mesh refinement study. Canva was used to design a report cover page.</p>
        <p>The final design significantly exceeded the minimum performance requirements for the assignment, and we achieved the best grade in the class (89%).</p>
      </div>

    <div class="card">
      <h2>Optimisation of a UAV Drone Frame for Wildfire Detection</h2>

      <div class="meta">
        <p><strong>Summary:</strong></p>
        <p>The objective of this project was to design and optimise a lightweight UAV drone frame capable of operating in extreme thermal environments encountered during wildfire detection and monitoring, while maintaining high structural stiffness and strength under thrust and manoeuvre loads.</p>
        <p>The design process involved establishing performance requirements, constructing a baseline CAD geometry, and iteratively refining the frame using finite element analysis (FEA). Structural and thermal mesh refinement studies were carried out to ensure numerical accuracy and mesh independence.</p>
        <p>The final UAV frame design achieved a significant mass reduction while maintaining a large safety margin against yielding and acceptable thermal performance.</p>
      </div>

    <div class="card">
      <h2>CFD Analysis of a NACA3412 Aerofoil</h2>

      <div class="meta">
        <p><strong>Summary:</strong></p>
        <p>This project investigated the aerodynamic performance of a NACA3412 aerofoil, both in its simple configuration and with a trailing-edge flap, using computational fluid dynamics (CFD).</p>
        <p>The geometry and mesh were constructed in ANSYS DesignModeler and Fluent Meshing, with near-wall refinement to achieve y<sup>+</sup> ≈ 1. Simulations were performed using the SST k–ω turbulence model.</p>
        <p>Lift, drag, and flow behaviour were analysed across multiple angles of attack and compared with theoretical and experimental reference data.</p>
      </div>

    <div class="card">
      <h2>Multibody Dynamics Analysis of an Engine Valvetrain</h2>

      <div class="meta">
        <p><strong>Summary:</strong></p>
        <p>This project investigated how valve spring stiffness affects valvetrain dynamics and engine performance at high RPM in an internal combustion engine.</p>
        <p>A multibody dynamics model of a cam–pushrod–rocker–valve system was developed in SolidWorks Motion Analysis using penalty-based solid-body contact. Valve kinematics, contact forces, and transient oscillations were analysed for stock and performance valve springs.</p>
        <p>Simulation data were exported and post-processed in MATLAB to evaluate contact forces, valve lift, velocity, acceleration, and the influence of damping on oscillation suppression.</p>
      </div>
